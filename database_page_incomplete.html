Databases
A database is a collection of relavent data which is organised.

A database can be paper-based (made of paper) or computerized (on a computer).
Database Pros Cons
Paper-based Not very expensive, doesn't require electricity to work. Paper can be lost or damaged, difficult to edit, only one person can use at once.
Computerized Easy to edit, many people can access a document at once, less likely to misplace data, can be easier to navigate. Data can be corrupted or subject to data rot, which can cause files to be lost.
A field is a single piece of data in a database. A record is a group or collection of fields regarding a specific person or thing.

Databases can store strings, integers, real numbers, boolean values, autonumbers, and more.

Some numbers, e.g. phone numbers, are strings, as you may need special characters such as "+" for an area code, and some phone numbers are too large of an integer for a computer to comprehend.
