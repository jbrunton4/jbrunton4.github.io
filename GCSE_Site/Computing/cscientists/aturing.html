Alan Turing
Background Information Alan Turing was a mathematician and computer scientist born in London 1912. He is most well known for making the bombe, although he also participated in other research. He was a big help in winning World War II.
Bombe One of Turing's most famous inventions was the bombe. In World War II, Germany used a method of encryption known as enigma to send their messages. This was very difficult to crack without a key if intercepted, but Turing made a machine to decrypt enigma, allowing the British to predict German attacks and prevent them.
Life After War While Turing was alive, homosexuality was still illegal - he was given the choice to be killed, or take a "cure". He chose the "cure", which made him depressed, which lead to (people think) him taking his own life via cyanide poisioning at the age of 41.
